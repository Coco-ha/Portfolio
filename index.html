    I'm a full-stack software engineer with a background in healthcare and bio-chemistry sciences. My journey is quite a long and twisted route. I first intended to go to college in pursuit of becoming a physcian, I soon reached my goal by being accepted into medical school after completing my Masters, but my interest in software development kept me from continuing this route. The thought of pursuing software came during my time as a biochemistry research intern at The University of Texas Arlington, I studied F420 Cofactor enzymes and processed data using spectrophotometry and other equipment. I was fascinated with the abundant amount of equipment and software that allowed us to be capable of studying tiny enzymes that our eyes and hands couldn't possible grasp. I quickly became curious as to how these equipment and software was built. Curiosity allowed me to plunge head-first into the world of software engineering, with the intent of oneday gaining the skills and knowledge to contribute to incredible software that can impact the abundant world of STEM.
